# Exercise 4.7 (monotonicity)

Observe that the sale probability $\Pr[\text{sale at price $p$}]$ is monotonically non-increasing in $p$. Use this monotonicity property to derive one-sided Lipschitzness: $$ \displaystyle\mu(p)-\mu(p^{\prime})\leq p-p^{\prime}\quad\text{for any two prices $p>p^{\prime}$}. $$ (72)
