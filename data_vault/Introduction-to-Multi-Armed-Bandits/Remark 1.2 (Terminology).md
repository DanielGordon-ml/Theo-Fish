# Remark 1.2 (Terminology)

Since our definition of regret sums over all rounds, we sometimes call it *cumulative* regret. When/if we need to highlight the distinction between $R(T)$ and $\operatornamewithlimits{\mathbb{E}}[R(T)]$, we say *realized regret* and *expected regret$; but most of the time we just say “regret” and the meaning is clear from the context. The quantity $R(T)$ is sometimes called *pseudo-regret$ in the literature.
