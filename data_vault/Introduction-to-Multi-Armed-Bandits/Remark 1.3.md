# Remark 1.3

Thus, $\mu(a)$ lies in the known interval $[\bar{\mu}(a)-\mathtt{rad},\bar{\mu}(a)+\mathtt{rad}]$ with high probability. A known interval containing some scalar quantity is called the *confidence interval* for this quantity. Half of this interval’s length (in our case, $\mathtt{rad}$) is called the *confidence radius*.
