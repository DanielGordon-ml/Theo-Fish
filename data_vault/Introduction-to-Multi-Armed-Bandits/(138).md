# (138)

Dynamic pricing with a single product is a special case of $\mathtt{BwK}$ with two resources: time (i.e.,\xa0the number of customers) and supply of the product. Actions correspond to chosen prices $p$. If the price is accepted, reward is $p$ and resource consumption is $1$. Thus, the outcome vector is $\displaystyle\vec{o}_{t}=\begin{cases}(p,1,1)&\text{if price $p$ is accepted}\\ (0,0,1)&\text{otherwise}.\end{cases}$
