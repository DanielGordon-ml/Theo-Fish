# Remark 1.12

For a more formal terminology, consider a regret bound of the form $C\cdot f(T)$, where $f(\cdot)$ does not depend on the mean reward vector $\mu$, and the “constant” $C$ does not depend on $T$. Such regret bound is called *instance-independent* if $C$ does not depend on $\mu$, and *instance-dependent* otherwise.
