# Theorem 12.3

Consider random variables $X_{1},X_{2},\ldots\in[0,1]$ that are not necessarily independent or identically distributed. For each $i\in[n]$, posit a number $\mu_{i}\in[0,1]$ such that $$\displaystyle\operatornamewithlimits{\mathbb{E}}\left[X_{i}\mid X_{1}\in J_{1}\,,\ \ldots\ ,X_{i-1}\in J_{i-1}\right]=\mu_{i},$$ for any intervals $J_{1}\,,\ \ldots\ ,J_{i-1}\subset[0,1]$. Then Eq. (174) holds, with $\beta=4$.
