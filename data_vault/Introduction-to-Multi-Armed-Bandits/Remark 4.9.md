# Remark 4.9

Any $\epsilon$-covering gives rise to an $\epsilon$-mesh: indeed, just pick one arbitrary representative from each subset in the covering. Thus, there exists an $\epsilon$-mesh $S$ with $|S|=N_{\epsilon}(X)$. We define the covering numbers in terms of $\epsilon$-coverings (rather than more directly in terms of $\epsilon$-meshes) in order to ensure that $N_{\epsilon}(Y)\leq N_{\epsilon}(X)$ for any subset $Y\subset X$, and consequently $\mathtt{COV}_{c}(Y)\leq\mathtt{COV}_{c}(X)$. In words, covering numbers characterize complexity of a metric space, and a subset $Y\subset X$ cannot be more complex than $X$.
