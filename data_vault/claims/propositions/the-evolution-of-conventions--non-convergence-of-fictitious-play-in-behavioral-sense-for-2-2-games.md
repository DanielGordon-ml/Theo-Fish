# Non-convergence of fictitious play in behavioral sense for 2×2 games

In the Battle of the Sexes game (Example 2), if memory is unbounded as in fictitious play and the players miscoordinate on the first move, then they will continue to miscoordinate forever. The frequency distributions converge to the mixed Nash equilibrium in which both players yield with probability 1/(1 + √2), but their behaviors do not converge to the behavior specified by the mixed Nash equilibrium, because in each period their moves are correlated: either both yield or both do not yield.
