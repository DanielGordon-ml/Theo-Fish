# Proposition II.1

For any common-interest game, the optimal joint action $a^{\mathrm{opt}} \in \arg\max_{a \in \mathcal{A}} W(a)$ is a $k$-strong Nash equilibrium for any $k \in [n]$.


## Proof

To show existence, we can simply observe that $a^{\mathrm{opt}} \in \arg\max_{a \in \mathcal{A}} W(a)$ is a $k$-strong Nash equilibrium for any $k \in [n]$. Because $W(a^{\mathrm{opt}}) \geq W(a')$ for all $a' \in \mathcal{A}$, the global optimal satisfies $W(a^{\mathrm{opt}}) \geq W(a_{\Gamma}', a_{-\Gamma}^{\mathrm{opt}})$, $\forall a_{\Gamma}' \in \mathcal{A}_{\Gamma}$, $\Gamma \in \mathcal{C}_{[k]}$.
