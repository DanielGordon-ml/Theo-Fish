# Exercise 4.6(b)

Consider Lipschitz bandits on $([0,1]^d, \ell_2)$ for $d\in\mathbb{N}$, with $\mu(x)=\mu^*-\mathcal{D}(x,S)$ for a target set $S\subset X$. Then the zooming dimension of the problem instance (with a suitably chosen multiplier) is at most the covering dimension of $S$.
