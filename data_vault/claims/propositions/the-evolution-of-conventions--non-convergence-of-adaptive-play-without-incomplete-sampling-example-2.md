# Non-convergence of adaptive play without incomplete sampling (Example 2)

Without incomplete sampling (k = m), adaptive play may not converge to a pure Nash equilibrium. In the Battle of the Sexes game with payoffs (Yield, Yield) = (0,0), (Yield, Not Yield) = (1, √2), (Not Yield, Yield) = (√2, 1), (Not Yield, Not Yield) = (0,0), if k = m and the initial sequence of m plays consists entirely of miscoordinated plays (both yielded or both failed to yield in each period), then the players miscoordinate forever.
