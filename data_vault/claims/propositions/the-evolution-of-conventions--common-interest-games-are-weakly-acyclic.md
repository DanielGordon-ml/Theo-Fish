# Common interest games are weakly acyclic

Suppose that the agents have common interests: for every two strategy tuples s and s', either everyone prefers s to s' or everyone prefers s' to s. Assume further that no two strategy-tuples are payoff equivalent. Given an arbitrary strategy-tuple s that is not a Nash equilibrium, there exists some agent i who can do better by playing s'_i instead of s_i. Let s' = (s'_i, s_{-i}). If s' is not Nash equilibrium, there is some agent j who can do better by playing s''_j instead of s'_j. Let s'' = (s''_j, s'_{-j}), and so forth. At each step of this adjustment process everyone's utility increases, so the process cannot cycle and it must end at a pure strategy Nash equilibrium. Moreover, this equilibrium must be strict because of the assumption that no two strategy-tuples are payoff equivalent and the players have common interests.
