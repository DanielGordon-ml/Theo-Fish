# Example 3 — Least resistance path from h_1 to h_2

Suppose that the perturbed process is in state h_1. To exit to h_2, one agent must choose a sufficient number of 2's to cause the other agent to choose 2. Assume that the Column player chooses 2 at least k'' = [(1/8)k] times in succession. If Row samples these choices (plus k - k'' choices of strategy 1), then Row's best reply is also strategy 2. At this point there is a positive probability that the process will converge to h_2 with no further mistakes. (This relies on the assumption that k ≤ m/3.) It may be checked that this is the least number of mistakes to go from h_1 to h_2 by any route. Thus the least resistant path is direct in the sense that it only involves strategies 1 and 2.
