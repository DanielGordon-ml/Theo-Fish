# Proposition V.2 (Comparison with Healy)

Theorem I.1 improves upon Healy's Theorem V.3 in two ways: (1) our exponent is (1−λ)μ = γμ compared to γμ/(1+γδ/(1+δ)) ≤ γμ, and (2) our result allows arbitrary initial distribution φ (with a factor ‖φ/π‖_π) while Healy's requires starting from the stationary distribution.
