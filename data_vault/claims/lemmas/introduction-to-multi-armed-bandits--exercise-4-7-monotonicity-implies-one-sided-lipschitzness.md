# Exercise 4.7 (monotonicity implies one-sided Lipschitzness)

The sale probability $\Pr[\text{sale at price } p]$ is monotonically non-increasing in $p$. This monotonicity property implies one-sided Lipschitzness: $\mu(p)-\mu(p')\leq p-p'$ for any two prices $p>p'$.
