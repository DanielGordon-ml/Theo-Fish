# Irreducibility and Aperiodicity of P^ε

Let h and h' be two distinct states. If P^ε is in state h at time t, there is a positive probability that all players will experiment for m periods in succession. Thus there is a positive probability that the process arrives at state h' at time t + m, so P^ε is irreducible. It is aperiodic because the process can move from h to h in exactly m periods, and also in exactly m + 1 periods. Hence P^ε has a unique stationary distribution μ^ε satisfying the equation μ^ε P^ε = μ^ε.
