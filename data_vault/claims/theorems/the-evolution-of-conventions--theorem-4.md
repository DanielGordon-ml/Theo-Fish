# Theorem 4

Let $P^0$ be a stationary Markov process on the finite state space $X$ with recurrent communication classes $X_1, \ldots, X_J$. Let $P^\varepsilon$ be a regular perturbation of $P^0$, and let $\mu^\varepsilon$ be its unique stationary distribution for every small positive ε. Then: (i) as $\varepsilon \to 0$, $\mu^\varepsilon$ converges to a stationary distribution $\mu^0$ of $P^0$. (ii) $x$ is stochastically stable $(\mu_x^0 > 0)$ if and only if $x$ is contained in a recurrent class $X_j$ that minimizes $\gamma_j$.


## Proof

The proof is divided into two lemmas. Lemma 1 establishes statement (i) and characterizes stochastically stable states via arborescence problems in graph $G$. Lemma 2 shows that this characterization reduces to arborescence problems in the smaller graph $\mathcal{I}$. Together they establish Theorem 4.
